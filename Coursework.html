Coursework


The CUNY-BC China program offers the following possible courses (each for 3 credits):

  1. ARTD 3105: Development of the Silk Road* Syllabus
  2. ARTD 3108: Art of China Syllabus
  3. BUSN 3175: Asian Business Syllabus
  4. CASD 1619: Intercultural Communication** Syllabus
  5. CASD 2623: Business Communication (cross-cultural perspectives)Syllabus
  6. CHIN 1010: Beginners Chinese-Level I Syllabus
  7. CHIN 1020: Intensive Elementary Chinese-Level II Syllabus
  8. CHIN 1030: Intensive Intermediate Chinese-Level III Syllabus
  9. CHIN 2024: Advanced (Chinese) Language Skills Syllabus
  10. HIST 3005: The Shaping of the Modern World*** Syllabus
  11. HIST 3534: Revolutionary China Syllabus
  12. PHIL 3722: Asian Philosophy Syllabus
  13. CASD 7296X - Special Topic Course for Graduate Students Syllabus

*fulfills Pathways "Flexible Common Core (World Cultures & Global Issues" requirement
**fulfills Pathways International Cultural Competency requirement
***fulfills Pathways World Cultures & Global Issues requirement

Note: Students can enroll in 1-2 courses (with only one language course).

All courses are taught by Brooklyn College faculty who are experts in the areas of study.

The courses are subject to sufficient enrollment, approval by Brooklyn College, and faculty availability. Students make their course selections in the Application Form. The final decision about which courses and how many courses can be offered is made 1-2 weeks after the application deadline or about 2 months before departure.


E-Permit for CUNY Students outside Brooklyn College:


Students from other CUNY campuses (such as Baruch, Hunter, City, City Tech, Queens, Lehman, etc) will need to apply for an e-permit. But you do not apply for e-permit until about one week after the application deadline when we have by that time decided what courses will be offered. When applying to our program, you just make your course selections in the application form.

During the 3 weeks in China, instruction for the courses is delivered in several venues: site visits, lectures & discussions, and online interactions (e.g., portal.cuny.edu or the social media of WeChat). To ensure that students will be able to complete the coursework with quality and have time for self-exploration and side trips while in China, reading assignments are given a few weeks before departure and some online work also starts before departure.


Graduate Students, three possible options:


Option 1: Some graduate students decided to take an undergraduate class, as they only wanted to have the experience of studying in China.

Option 2: Some graduate students took an undergraduate class (with additional reading and research assignments), and got approval from their department and advisor to have it count as credit of graduate coursework.

Option 3: Graduate students may register for a graduate-level Special Topic/Independent Study course (e.g., CASD 7296X in communication if the student's department can accept the credit of the speech graduate-level course; or one graduate level special topic/independent study course in the student’s department with approval of his/her department chair or graduate studies director). That graduate-level special topic/independent study course is to be linked with one relevant undergraduate course offered during the program, but the graduate student(s) will be given extra reading assignments and research work.
